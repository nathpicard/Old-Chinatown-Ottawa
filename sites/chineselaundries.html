Chinese Laundries on Albert Street

Washing the clothes of the Nation's capital

Chinese laundromats were some of the first businesses established by local entrepreneurs moving from across the seas. The business was easy to get into, and soon earlier arrivals were hiring later ones to come work in their businesses. Over the course of the first half of the 20th century, the vast majority of chinese labourers in Ottawa worked in laundromats. Joe Shung, one of the first immigrants to the city, eventually came to own several laundromats across the city. Seeing as customers were in need of laundry all over Ottawa, we can see the establishment of Chinese laundries across the city to meet those needs. On Albert Street, two stayed in business consistently since 1931- On Hong Chinese Laundry and Song Lee. However, as the decades passed, and hand laundries became a thing of the past, so we can see a decline in the percentage of chinese in Ottawa who work in laundries. This was partly because the children of inital immigrants often sought an education and aimed for careers in government, or as doctors and lawyers. .
